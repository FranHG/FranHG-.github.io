The country of... Italy

Introduction

Italy officially the Italian Republic, is a member country of the European Union, whose form of government is a parliamentary republic. Its territory, with its capital in Rome, is divided into twenty regions, which in turn are made up of 110 provinces.

It has been home to many European cultures such as the Nuraghic civilization, the Etruscans, the Greeks, the Romans and was also the cradle of Humanism and the Renaissance, which began in the region of Tuscany and soon spread throughout Europe. The capital of Italy, Rome, has for centuries been the political and cultural centre of Western civilisation. Moreover, it is the holy city for the Catholic Church, the Pope being the bishop of Rome and the micro-state of the Vatican being within the city. The cultural significance of the country is reflected in all its World Heritage Sites, as it has 55, the highest number of them in the world.

It is the third country of the European Union that receives more tourists per year, being Rome the third most visited city.Other important cities are Milan, centre of finance and industry, and, according to the Global Language Monitor, the capital of fashion;8 Naples, important port in the Mediterranean, historical capital and most populated city in the Mezzogiorno; Turin, centre of the automotive industry and industrial design. Italy is a democratic republic, part of the G7 or group of the seven largest advanced nations in the world, and is a developed country with a very high quality of life, ranking in 2005 among the top seven in the world.

Best Places to visit

In this section I will try to show you some of the monuments and places to visit that are worth visiting in Italy:

  1. Venice:

    2. Venice is a city located in the northeast of Italy. It is also the capital of the Veneto region and the province of Venice. Its historical centre, declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO, is located on a group of islands in the Venice lagoon, in the northern part of the Adriatic Sea. Its geographical particularities, as well as its thousand-year-old history and rich monumental and artistic heritage, have made Venice one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world.

  2. Pisa:

    3. Pisa is a city in the Italian region of Tuscany, capital of the province of the same name. Among the city's most important monuments is the cathedral, built in marble between 1064 and 1118 in the Pisan Romanesque style, with its bronze portal by Bonanno Pisano and the pulpit by Giovanni Pisano. Next to the cathedral is the striking 12th century leaning tower, 58.36 metres high, which underwent its characteristic inclination immediately after construction began.

    Due to the nature of the terrain, there are two other leaning towers in Pisa: the bell tower of the Church of San Nicola at the opposite end of Via Santa Maria along the Lungarno; and the bell tower of the Church of San Michele degli Scalzi in the middle of the river promenade of the Piagge in the eastern part of the city (in this case the church is also leaning). The Palazzo Toscanini in "Lungarno Pacinotti" is also leaning.

  3. Florence:

    4. Florence is a city located in the north of the central region of Italy, the capital and most populated city of the homonymous metropolitan city and of the region of Tuscany, of which it is the historical, artistic, economic and administrative center. It has about 378,239 inhabitants, and is the centre of a metropolitan area of approximately one and a half million people.

    Florence is the urban centre where the artistic movement known as the Renaissance originated in the second half of the 14th century, and is considered one of the world's cradles of art and architecture as well as one of the most beautiful cities in the world. Its historic centre was declared a World Heritage Site in 19823 and features medieval and Renaissance works such as the dome of Santa Maria del Fiore, the Ponte Vecchio, the Basilica of Santa Croce, the Palazzo Vecchio and museums such as the Uffizi, the Bargello and the Accademia Gallery, which houses Michelangelo's David.

  4. Rome:

    5. Rome is an Italian city of 2 857 321 inhabitants, capital of the region of Lazio and Italy. It is the most populated municipality in Italy and the third most populated city in the European Union, and has been known since ancient times as the Urbe (Urbs). It is also called "The Eternal City"

    In the course of its history, which spans three millennia, it came to extend its dominion over the entire Mediterranean basin and much of Europe, the Middle East and North Africa. As the capital of the Roman Empire, it became the first great metropolis of humanity, the centre of one of the most important ancient civilizations. It influenced the society, culture, language, literature, music, art, architecture, philosophy, gastronomy, religion, law and morals of the following centuries.

    It is the city with the highest concentration of historical and architectural assets in the world; its historical center, delimited by the perimeter marked by the Aurelian walls, overlapping traces of three millennia, is the highest expression of the historical, artistic and cultural heritage of the Western world. In 1980, along with the extraterritorial properties of the Holy See located in the city and the Basilica of St. Paul Outside the Walls, it was included in the list of UNESCO's World Heritage Sites.

  5. The Vatican:

    6. The Vatican City, officially the Vatican City State or simply the Vatican, is a landlocked sovereign state whose territory consists of an enclave within the city of Rome on the Italian peninsula. It is one of the six European micro-states, and is also the smallest country in the world in terms of area and population.

    The architectural and historical-artistic complex that makes up the Vatican City was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1984.
