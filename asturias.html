Introduction

Asturias The Principality of Asturias is a uniprovincial autonomous community of Spain, with a population of 1,028,244 inhabitants. Bathed in the north by the waters of the Cantabrian Sea, bordered on the west by the province of Lugo (Galicia), on the south by the province of Leon (Castilla y Leon) and east by Cantabria. It is called the Principality for historical reasons, as the heir to the Crown of Castilla and, by extension, the Crown of Spain, holds the noble title of 'Prince of Asturias'.

Best Places to visit

In this section I will try to show you some of the monuments and places to visit that are worth visiting in Asturias:

  1. Oviedo:

    2. Oviedo is a council and Spanish city, capital of the Principality of Asturias. Its origin dates back to the High Middle Ages, or even before, and was called Ovetum. It is also an Asturian council whose capital is the city of the same name, Oviedo, and a parish of that council, which includes Oviedo capital.

    It is the commercial, university, religious and administrative centre of the Principality, the seat of the General Assembly of the Principality of Asturias, the official institutions of the Principality of Asturias, the University of Oviedo, the Museum of Fine Arts of Asturias, the Princess of Asturias Awards and the Archdiocese of Oviedo. It is recognized as one of the cities with the highest quality of life in Europe according to the European Commission.

    The municipality of Oviedo is the second most populated in the autonomous community, with 219,686 inhabitants.

  2. Gijón:

    3. Gijón, is a Spanish city, with the historical category of town, capital of the council of the same name. The city is located on the shores of the Cantabrian Sea on the coast of the Principality of Asturias, an autonomous community of which the municipality is the most populous with 271 843 inhabitants.

    From its foundation in the 5th century BC until recently, it had been an eminently industrial town, which favoured its great development and growth during the 20th century. However, in recent years the crisis in the iron and steel industry and the naval sector has led to the conversion of its productive fabric, which has transformed the city into an important tourist, university, commercial and R+D+I centre.
